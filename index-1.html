This short guide covers five essential ideas to communicate politely online (Netiquette) and to keep email accounts secure. Each point includes a short explanatory paragraph followed by practical bullet tips for quick action.

Five Main Points

1. Respect & Tone

Treat others politely online—tone can be misread. Clear, respectful messages reduce conflicts and build trust.

  • Use greetings and simple courtesies.
  • Avoid sarcasm or ALL CAPS.
  • Pause before replying to heated messages.

2. Clarity & Purpose

State your purpose early—help readers understand your message quickly.

  • Use clear subject lines.
  • Break messages into short paragraphs.
  • Include clear next steps.

3. Privacy & Consent

Respect personal information—always ask before sharing anything private.

  • Ask permission before forwarding messages.
  • Use BCC for group emails.
  • Do not share private data without consent.

4. Account Security

Protect accounts with strong passwords and extra verification steps.

  • Use long passphrases.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication.
  • Use a password manager.

5. Phishing & Links

Many attacks use fake links—always verify before clicking.

  • Inspect links before tapping.
  • Don’t open unexpected attachments.
  • Check for spelling errors and mismatched sender details.
